Cognitive Science: Research Projects

Modeling the Emergence of "Shared Attention"


Under Dr. Gedeon Deak, the MESA study was created to track the development of shared attention in infants. This is a Neurolinguisitcs lab and we use multiple techniques to accurately model the development of this behavior. An accurate pattern of development will aid in early diagnosis of disorders like Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) which is characterised by impaired social interaction and communication.

Kalvi Institute of Mind and Brain

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder-like Disgust Sensations Arise from Dummy during Rubber Hand Illusion


Under Dr. V.S. Ramachandran, we investigated the relationship between Mirror Neurons and Obsessive Compulsive Disorder. We did this by exploring disgust sensations using somatosensory illusions. In this study, we explore whether OCD-like disgust may result from contamination of a dummy hand; suggesting a possible integration of somatosensory and limbic inputs in the construction of body image.

The Brain and Perception Laboratory
University of California, San Diego.

Case Study

Exploring the Emergence of Cognitive Architecture in the "Dabbawalla" System, a massive social entrepreneurship organization.

Who They Are

A “dabbawala”, literally translated to “tiffin delivery man”, is a person who collects hot food in lunch boxes from residences and delivers it to the workplace of their customers everyday. Between 175,000 and 200,000 lunch boxes are moved by 4,500 to 5,000 dabbawalas for a fee of about $8 per month.

Abstract

This is an exploration of the various mechanisms of social coordination to distribute tasks and information across people and time. The result is an emergent system property that is able to accomplish this incredibly complex task.
